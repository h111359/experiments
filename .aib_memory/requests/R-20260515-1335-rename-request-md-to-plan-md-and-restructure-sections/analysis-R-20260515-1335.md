### Industry Knowledge

**Finding 1 ā€” Separation of Concerns in technical specifications (IEEE 29148, ISO/IEC 25010):**
IEEE 29148 recommends distinguishing stakeholder requirements specifications (human-review artifacts) from system requirements specifications (execution artifacts). A mature engineering pattern is to maintain two document types per work item: a contextual specification for stakeholders and a work order for executors. AIB's reform directly applies this: `analysis-<ID>.md` is the stakeholder document; `plan-<ID>.md` becomes the executor specification. Applicability: high. The new 4-section plan schema aligns with the "system requirements specification" concept ā€” terse, bounded, machine-interpretable.

**Finding 2 ā€” Executable specifications in BDD/TDD practice (Gherkin/Cucumber):**
The BDD community distinguishes "specifications by example" (human-readable shared understanding artifacts) from "step definitions" (precise machine-executable structures). Gherkin's Given-When-Then structure excludes explanatory prose at the execution level; scope is expressed implicitly through exhaustive enumeration of scenarios rather than boundary statements. This directly supports removing `## Scope` and relying on fully self-contained WBS Procedure steps (Q001 Option A). Applicability: strong alignment with the chosen approach.
